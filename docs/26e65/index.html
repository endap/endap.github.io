The sine function is one of the primary trigonometric function 1, which has a unique property 2. It can be approximated using series, e.g. Maclaurin series, which is Taylor series when 0 is the point where the derivatives are considered 3. Sine general formula for Maclaurin series 4, graphical contribution of considered terms 5, and C code 6 are available. Here derivate of $\sin x$ with respect to $x$ in the form of Maclaurin series are presented.

series

$$ \tag{1} \sin x = x - \frac{1}{3!} x^3 + \frac{1}{5!} x^5 - \frac{1}{7!} x^7 + \dots. $$

$$ \tag{2} \cos x = 1 - \frac{1}{2!} x^2 + \frac{1}{4!} x^4 - \frac{1}{6!} x^6 + \dots. $$

derivative

$$ \tag{3} \begin{array}{rcl} \displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \sin x & = & \displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \left( x - \frac{1}{3!} x^3 + \frac{1}{5!} x^5 - \frac{1}{7!} x^7 + \dots \right) \newline \newline & = & \displaystyle 1 - \frac{3}{3!} x^2 + \frac{5}{5!} x^4 - \frac{7}{7!} x^6 + \dots \newline \newline & = & \displaystyle 1 - \frac{1}{2!} x^2 + \frac{1}{4!} x^4 - \frac{1}{6!} x^6 + \dots \newline \newline & = & \cos x. \end{array} $$

notes

  • The relation $\frac{d}{dx} \sin x = \cos x$ can also be obtained using Taylor series.
  • Using the similar way shown above the relation $\frac{d}{dx} \cos x = - \sin x$ is obtainable.
  • It also works for $\frac{d}{dx} \sin ax = a \cos ax$ and $\frac{d}{dx} \cos ax = -a \sin ax$.
